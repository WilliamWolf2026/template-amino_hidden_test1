# City Lines — Narrative Guide

This document defines the narrative framework for *City Lines*, showing how character, tone, and progression support news stories within the game's structure.

It explains how weekly articles are experienced through chapter flow and companion moments, and includes a fallback narrative approach for times when news content is unavailable.

---

## Goodboi the News Hound

A News Hound who sniffs out developing stories across town. Each story is scattered in pieces, and you track them by connecting roads that lead from neighborhood to neighborhood.

---

## Description

Goodboi is a curious News Hound who uncovers stories by going where they show up around town. Each story is scattered across different locations, and every place holds a small piece of the article. When the roads are connected, Goodboi can reach those locations and pick up the next part of the story.

He moves from neighborhood to neighborhood, focused and eager, gathering the article one piece at a time. By visiting every key spot, Goodboi helps bring the full story together in a way that feels natural, grounded, and easy to follow.

---

## Character Traits

- **Personality:** Curious, alert, friendly, determined
- **Motivation:** Follow the strongest story scent and uncover what is happening
- **Behavior:** Tracks stories by moving from neighborhood to neighborhood
- **Tone:** Warm, playful, observant, never cynical

---

## Connection to Player

Players connect the roads on the map to guide Goodboi from one location to the next. Each completed route allows him to reach a new place where another piece of the story has surfaced.

By choosing how the roads connect, the player determines where Goodboi can go and which part of the article is uncovered next. Connecting all locations reveals the full story.

---

## News Distribution

Each section of the game is built around a single news story, broken into smaller pieces and spread across multiple locations in town. Each level reveals one piece of that same story.

As players connect roads and open new routes, Goodboi can reach the next location and uncover the next part of the article. After all levels in the section are completed, every piece comes together and the full news story is revealed.

---

## Chapter Overview

A chapter is the primary narrative and gameplay unit. Each chapter is built around a single real New Jersey news story and unfolds across multiple puzzle levels, each anchored to a specific county.

The player uncovers the story gradually by completing levels, with the full article revealed only at the end of the chapter.

---

## Content Generation

### 1. Story Backbone

- One real news article selected per day
- Broken into 5–10 short story clues
- Clues progress from vague to specific
- Full article revealed only upon chapter completion

### 2. Geographic Scope

- Chapters may be:
    - County-based (all levels in one county), or
    - Statewide (levels span multiple counties)
- Each level is always tied to one specific New Jersey county

### 3. Introduction Screen

- *"Hi there! I'm Goodboi. I sniff out news all over town, but the stories come in pieces. Let's connect the paths and figure out what's going on!"*

### 4. Section Start (Narrative Hook)

- This is where the story framing actually happens
- Delivered by Goodboi before Level 1
- Short, playful, intrigue-driven
- Hints at the subject without revealing it

### 5. Level Sequence

- 5–10 levels per chapter
- Each level includes:
    - A road-connection puzzle
    - A county-specific setting
    - One story clue on completion
- All levels contribute to the same article

### 6. Visual & Thematic Layer

- Each level uses county-specific flavor:
    - Backgrounds or landmarks
    - Decorations and color shifts

### 7. Chapter Completion Interstitial

*Triggered only after the final level.*

**Displays:**

- Full headline
- 2–3 sentence summary
- Featured image
- County or statewide attribution
- Bookmark option
- Confirmation text: *"Continue to Next Chapter"*
- External link to the full article

### 8. Progression Reset

- After completion:
    - A new chapter loads
    - A new story begins
    - Progress resets to 0
- The loop supports **endless, weekly content**

### 9. Chapter Length & Pacing

- Chapter length varies based on article depth and complexity
- Chapters may contain:
    - **5 levels** for shorter or single-focus stories
    - **7 levels** for standard news coverage
    - **10 levels** for longer or more detailed reporting
- Each level delivers one meaningful piece of the same story
- Regardless of length, the full article is revealed only after the final level

## Fallback Patrol Chapters

Fallback patrol chapters are intentionally generic and modular.

They are not tied to specific locations, landmarks, or real-world events, allowing the experience to run independently of weekly news content.

---

### Goodboi on Patrol

Goodboi is a loyal neighborhood dog who makes regular patrols through town. He knows the streets, the corners, and the places people gather. When roads are broken or routes don't connect, parts of his territory become unreachable. That's when Goodboi needs help.

As he moves from area to area, Goodboi checks in on familiar places, making sure everything feels right again. His goal isn't to solve a mystery or uncover a story — it's to make the whole neighborhood feel connected and complete.

---

### Patrol Character Traits

- **Personality:** Loyal, curious, observant, routine-driven
- **Motivation:** Complete his patrol and make sure every part of the neighborhood is reachable
- **Behavior:** Moves methodically from place to place, checking familiar spots once roads are connected
- **Tone:** Warm, upbeat, attentive, quietly determined

---

### Patrol Connection to Player

The player helps Goodboi complete his patrol.

By rotating and connecting roads, the player reopens paths between familiar spots. Each completed connection allows Goodboi to continue forward, reclaiming another stretch of his daily route.

The player isn't directing him toward a mystery or objective. They're simply helping him move freely through the neighborhood again.

---

### Patrol Chapter Overview

A chapter represents one patrol route through a region.

- Each chapter is made up of 7 levels
- Each level restores access to another part of the neighborhood
- The chapter focuses on completing Goodboi's patrol of that area

At the start of a chapter, part of Goodboi's patrol is blocked. As levels are completed, more of the route becomes reachable. When the final level is finished, the patrol loop is fully restored.

There is no story reveal. The reward is completion: the satisfaction of seeing the entire neighborhood connected and Goodboi free to roam his full route again.

---

### Patrol Structure

#### 1. Patrol Backbone

- Each chapter represents one complete patrol loop
- The patrol is broken into 7 short dialogue beats
- Dialogue progresses from blocked route → fully restored loop
- There is no story reveal

The reward is spatial completion, not narrative discovery.

#### 2. Geographic Scope

- Chapters are fully location-agnostic
- No counties, landmarks, or real-world references
- Environments are abstract and modular
- Levels represent generic stretches of a neighborhood route

#### 3. Introduction Screen

- A reusable greeting from Goodboi, with variants
- Establishes that part of his patrol route is blocked
- Not tied to any specific place or event

Example tone:

> "Hey. I'm Goodboi. Part of my patrol isn't connecting today. Let's fix that."

#### 4. Section Start

- Delivered before Level 1
- Signals that the route feels incomplete
- Short, warm, forward-moving
- No specific references

Purpose: establish momentum.

#### 5. Level Sequence

- Each chapter contains 7 levels
- Each level includes:
    - A road-connection puzzle
    - One short patrol dialogue line
- Each line reflects incremental progress in restoring the route

Progression arc:

- Early levels: noticing gaps
- Mid levels: reconnecting stretches
- Final levels: nearing completion

#### 6. Visual Layer

- Environments are abstract
- No geographic identifiers
- Subtle layout and color variation only
- Focus remains on route restoration

#### 7. Chapter Completion

- Triggered after final level
- One upbeat patrol-complete line from Goodboi

Tone: celebratory but simple.

Example:

> "Woof! That's a full patrol. Every path's open!"

#### 8. Progression Reset

- After completion:
    - A new patrol begins
    - Progress resets to 0
- Designed for infinite replayability
- Independent of editorial schedule
